The story of junk mails

Traditionally, businesses have been collecting mailing addresses from their customers as a way to communicate or to promote their products / services. In certain countries such as US, however, businesses sell or share your mailing addresses to other businesses so you suddenly become a target audience for some random company that you might have not even heard of before. The tactic that businesses use is essentially throw garbage at everyone and hope that someone finds interest in them. It’s also unfortunate that as a consumer, we would have to actually opt out from receiving them even if we did not opt in to begin with.

What you can do (if you are in the US)

There are a couple services that you can use to stop getting these junk mails.

  • CatalogChoice is a non-profit organization that provides a service to stop junk mails. It is free, but it doesn’t do much other than redirect you to the businesses’ websites.
  • DMAChoice has similar service and they claim that 80% of US households read some or all of their members’ advertising mails. They charge $2 for you to unsubscribe from their catalogs, and it is only for ten years. (See how their model works?)
  • National Do Not Mail List provides the same service for free. You just need to submit your information once.

Last but not least, Junk Mail Be Gone is a chrome extension that helps you unsubscribe from catalogs individually, and guides you through the various forms on different websites. Right now it only has limited number of catalogs (The starting list is the catalogs I personally receive the most junk mails from).

With the amount of advertisements you get on TV and the Internet, do you really need more advertisements in your mailbox?
